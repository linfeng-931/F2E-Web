How do plastic bags impact seabirds and marine mammals?

Plastic bags are a very convenient daily item for humans, but they pose a threat to marine creatures.

Plastic bags are common items in our daily lives, but they are very difficult to decompose and manage. When they end up in the ocean, they can be mistakenly eaten by creatures like sea turtles and whale sharks, thinking they are food.

WHALE SHARK

The whale shark is the largest fish in the world, primarily inhabiting tropical waters, and it can live for up to seventy years. Interestingly, its name comes from the fact that it is a shark that is as large as a whale.

ALBATROSS

The albatross is a seabird that primarily inhabits the Southern Ocean and the North Pacific. They hunt for fish and shrimp on the ocean surface, which is also a primary reason they accidentally ingest marine debris. They are currently listed as an endangered species.
